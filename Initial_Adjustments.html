Initial Adjustments:

For initial adjustments I recommend the following:

  • Disconnect ribbon cable between the OS and FCFE PCBs.
  • If possible,  connect power to the amplifier through a Variac. 

Output Stage Adjustment:

  • Trimmer P4 adjusts Vds, the voltage between drain and source, of the 2SK182ES SIT.    What is particularly important the the voltage between node S, the SIT source pin, and ground be within the voltage range of the output electrolytic capacitor C5.   There are two alternative power configurations:
    • positive and negative rails: The voltage at Node S should be negative at around -2V to -5V.
    • ground and negative rails: The voltage at Node S should be negative at around 1/2 the voltage of the negative rail.
  • Trimmer P5 adjusts the OS idle current, which is determined by the voltage across the IXTN40PN50P PFET drain sense resistor(s) R22.    For idle current Ibias, the voltage across R22 should be V(R22) = 0.097*Ibias.  In the prototype SIT3X, I have adjusted for V(R22)=180mV, thus Ibias= 1.856A.   DiyAudio Store 5U 400mm chassis, the maximul value for Ibias is estimated to be about 1.6A or V(R22)=0.155V.

Start with the P4 and P5 CCW, assuming the trimmers are correctly oriented.  P5 center tap to the Opto LED anode resistance should be near zero.  P4 center tap to top of R24 should be near zero.

Two voltage measurements must be monitored while adjusting P4 and P5, and is best done using two multimeters.   Connect one multimeter between V(S) the DC voltage between the SIT source pin, and ground.  Connect the other multimeter across R22.  These adjustments strongly interact with a fairly long time constant.    

  1. Apply power through the Variac initially at a low voltage and increase while monitoring the two voltage measurements.  The main thing to look for is that the idle current is nearly zero with P4 in the CCW position (or the CW position if the trimmer is backwards).   The voltage at node S should be near zero.   If these voltages look good, slowly increase the Variac output voltage until the nominal line voltage is reached.   
  2. With the amplifier powered at normal AC line voltage, alternately turn P4 and P5 in the CW direction with the goal being to achieve V(S) = -2V to -5V, and V(R22)=0.097*Ibias.

Folded Cascode Front End Adjustment:

The FCFE has two trimmers P3FE and P4FE which adjust the currents through the resistors R31 and R32, which ultimately adjust the FCFE M1FE and M2FE FET idle currents.    Those currents are determined by measuring the voltages across the 22 Ohm resistors R10 and R11, which should be installed in a manner that permits the attachment of multimeter probes.  The idle current of M1FE is V(R10)/22R.  In the SIT3X prototype, I chose a idle current of 50mA, but it can be somewhat lower. At 50mA, V(R10)=1.1V.

With the power off, connect the ribbon cable between the OS and FCFE PCBs.  State with P3FE and P4FE in the fully CCW where the resistance of the wiper to the end of R35b/R36b is near zero Ohms.  

Connect one multimeter across R10 or R11, and connect the other multimeter between FEOut and ground.  The adjustment goal to achieve near zero volts at FEOut and a value near 1.1V across R(10). 

  1. Apply power to the amplifier.
  2. Alternately adjust P3FE and P4FE.  The adjustment goal to achieve near zero volts at FEOut and a value near 1.1V across R(10).  This is an iterative process since these adjustments strongly interact.

X Pot Initial Adjustment:

The X pot, P6 on the OS PCB, adjusts AC signal level to the PFET relative to that of the SIT, and has a major effect on the behavior of the output stage.  For a variety of reasons I have chosen a particular adjustment of P6 that both sounds good to me and is easily achieved without needing a spectrum analyzer or other special equipment.    This is the value where at low output wattage the PFET is acting as a current source and the SIT is doing all of the real work, as in a single-ended output stage.
  1. Connect an 8 Ohm load of at least 3 Watts to the the amplifier output.
  2. Apply a 1kHz (the frequency can be as low of 60Hz) sine wave signal of around 0.7Vpeak (0.5Vrms) to the amplifier input.
  3. Connect a AC voltmeter to across the R22, the PFET drain sense resistor.
  4. Apply power to the amplifier.
  5. Adjust P6 for as close to zero Vac as possible.  The value should be less than 1mVrms.
