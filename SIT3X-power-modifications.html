Since the SIT3X prototype uses the same power supply for both the OS and the FCFE,  the FCFE rail voltages are also reduced.  Because of the design of the folded cascode and the additional degenerated source follower buffer, the FCFE output swing is limited to about 8V less than the rail voltages.    In order to obtain 50W into 8R the FCFE needs at least +/-40V rails.   But the OS only needs about +34V/-34V rails.  By removing the capacitance multiplier and grounding the SIT drain the OS only needs 0/-64V rails as shown in the schematic below.   I have bench tested the modifications with +/-34V OS rails (28VAC transformer secondaries) and +/-44V FCFE rails and all works well.
